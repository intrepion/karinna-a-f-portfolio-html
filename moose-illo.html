Skate-Squatch Illustration

Personal Project
Skate-Squatch Illustration

This was a personal project I did to practice developing my personal illustration style. I came up with the idea for this Sasquatch skateboarding character while looking through my sketchbook. I drew the piece by hand, then scanned it into the computer and made the final digital illustration in Photoshop, using a Wacom tablet and stylus. My process for digital illustration is to make all the basic grayscale shapes, then choose the main color scheme, then add light and shadows, and the final step is to add texture and small details.

Scentsy Flyer of the Month

Scentsy
My Role
I worked as a junior designer for Scentsy during the 2016 Winter Holiday Season, under senior designers Casey Joplin and Jeremy Ryan who work under the Art Direction of Kris Crimmins. I assisted on a variety of projects during my time at Scentsy, such as photography set design, pattern design for wax packaging, making illustrations for various projects, logo design concepts, and editing the catalog, product information sheets, and consultant training manual into French, Spanish, German, and Queens English versions. I was also on the team that designed the March 2017 Warmer of the month flyer.

Flyer of the Month Team
One of the projects I had a bigger role in helping develop was the March 2017 Warmer of the Month flyer for Scentsys new Lawn Gnome Warmer and Ocean Island scented wax. I was on a team with graphic designers Vy Bouty-Tatum, Richelle Lynn, Danny Rosas, and Jeremy Ryan. As well as, Photographer, Aaron Rodriquez, and Production Manager, Sarah Ellen.

Problem/Goal
Our task was to create a Flyer that showcased the lawn gnome-shaped warmer and their new wax scent called Blue Grotto. Our goal was to make a cohesive flyer for two things that didnt seem very related - a lawn gnome and a tropical scent.

Experience
We began our process with mood boarding and sketches, then presenting our ideas to the art director and CEO, revising the concepts based on our feedback, and then presenting the changes. The concept that got approved was geared towards millennials. Lots of millennials live in apartments, many dont have yards but they probably have house plants, so the lawn gnome would be hanging out in an apartment garden. Once the concept was approved we moved forward with more sketches, then digital mock-ups. Richelle and I went shopping with the production manager to buy props, and on the day of the photoshoot, we set up the indoor garden and worked with the photographer to get the perfect shot. After the photographs were edited and sent back to us we put together the final flyer, which went through multiple rounds of edits before being sent to print.

Outcome 
In the end, I think we successfully executed the CEOs vision. The indoor garden looks humble yet sophisticated, we used the pattern from the waxs packaging for the wallpaper in the background, and photoshopped cross-stitched letters onto an embroidery ring that was hanging in the photograph to give the scene a homey feel. The end project came across as fun, bright, and appealing. Im very proud of the work our team did, and the great experience I gained from working at Scentsy.

Bay Area Used Bikes Homepage

Bay Area Used Bikes
My Role
As a web designer at Dealer Spike, it was my responsibility to meet with clients over the phone, assess their needs, and design a homepage mockup based on our conversation. Each graphic designer at Dealer Spike works individually and is responsible for project managing their own clients before passing them on to the production team where their site gets built. When designing our clients homepages we needed to use Bootstraps grid systems and use the UI best practices that Dealer Spike has developed such as prominent contact info in the header, and call to action buttons above the fold of a website. We had very limited time to spend on each client, especially during busy periods where we were each juggling as many as 15 clients at a time. I had to work quickly and efficiently in order to reach deadlines.

Problem/Goal
The challenge presented for this project was to create a dynamic homepage for Bay Area Used Bikes from start to finish in roughly 8 hours. They wanted their site to have an urban, street bike style, but wanted to make sure that potential customers could tell they also sell dirt bikes just by skimming the homepage. They wanted to make sure that this site was different from their sister stores website and also wanted to be able to highlight their shipping services for customers outside of California. Dealer Spikes ultimate goal is to increase a dealerships monthly leads. Most of those leads come from Dealer Spikes marketing services, but web design also played a role in reaching this goal by providing clients with easy to navigate websites that offer quick ways to get into the shop inventory pages. This ensures that there is less bounce (a customer leaving a site out of frustration because they cant find something), and that customers stay on the dealerships website longer.

Experience
After my consultation with Bay Area Used Bikes, I went over my notes and made quick sketches of their homepage layout. During this phase of my process, I am deciding which features would be the most beneficial to the dealership, and what order would make the most sense to a potential customer. Since they are a used motorcycle dealership, I put shop-by-price buttons above the fold. Other dealerships that sell exclusively used vehicles have seen a lot of success when shop by price range buttons are the first thing their customers interact with. Customers shopping used are often looking for a bargain, and the price range buttons are very attractive to bargain hunters. Bay Area Used Bikes wanted to be able to feature promotions and specials they could easily update themselves, so I put a slideshow above the shop by price buttons. Other elements the client asked for were shop-by-model buttons, for the customer who already knows what model they want, and a featured inventory where the dealership could feature bikes that were on clearance. After I completed my sketches, I began researching styles for the overall look and feel and put together a quick mood board. During this part of my process, I also gathered stock photos and textures I needed. Because this project had such a quick turnaround time, one way I sped up my process was to use widgets and elements from other sites Ive already made and then tweak them to match the style of this site.

Outcome
Im very happy with the outcome of the homepage design. The page scrolls over a fixed image background that I made using concrete texture and a street bike image. The grunge texture I used to separate different sections looks like muddy tire tracks which I felt was a reference to dirt bikes. During the revision process with the client, they decided they wanted the shop by model section to also function as a sticky footer that can be hidden if the customer chooses. The sticky footer has icons of dirt bikes and street bikes and is always above the fold, thus their customers can tell from a quick glance that they sell both kinds of motorcycles which addressed one of the clients main concerns.
